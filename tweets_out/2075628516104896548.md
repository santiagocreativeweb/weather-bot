Multi-Model Weighting on Polymarket Weather: How to Properly Weight ECMWF, GEFS and HRRR for Maximum Edge 

Most traders take a simple average of all models and think that’s good enough

Top accounts weight models differently - depending on city, lead time, and season

This is one of the most underrated ways to improve your probabilities

> Why Blind Average Performs Poorly

- ECMWF is often stronger on 48–72h horizon  
- HRRR / GEFS shine on short horizons (0–36h)  
- The same model can have very different skill in different cities  
- Summer and winter biases change

Simple averaging smooths out strengths and amplifies weaknesses

> How to Do Weighting Properly

Start simple and scale up:

1. Basic approach (good for starters)

Build a static table: for each city and horizon assign higher weight to the historically strongest model (e.g. HRRR for D+0 in US cities, ECMWF for D+2)

2. Better approach (recommended)

Use dynamic weighting based on recent performance - last 30–60 days accuracy per city. The model that performed best lately gets more weight

3. Pro level

Implement stacking or Bayesian weighting - a small meta-model that learns the best combination on the fly

Even moving from equal weights to a simple per-city table already gives a noticeable lift in probability accuracy

> Real-World Impact

On narrow 1–2°C buckets, proper weighting can shift a bucket’s fair probability by 6–12%

That directly means more +EV trades and higher win-rate.Many consistently profitable bots use weighted ensembles instead of raw averages

If you’re still giving equal weight to every model - you’re leaving one of the cleanest repeatable edges on the table
